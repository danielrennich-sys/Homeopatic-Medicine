What Are LM Potencies?

LM potencies (also called Q-potencies or 50 millesimal potencies) are a method of homeopathic remedy preparation developed by Samuel Hahnemann in the final years of his life and published in the 6th edition of the Organon of Medicine (written 1842, published posthumously in 1921).

The "LM" stands for the Roman numeral 50,000 — the dilution ratio used in preparation (1:50,000), compared to the centesimal (C) scale's 1:100 ratio. This creates a fundamentally different therapeutic action: gentler, deeper, and suitable for daily repetition without the aggravations often seen with centesimal potencies.

"This most perfect of all methods of treating chronic diseases is the most recent discovery and is practiced by only a few careful homoeopaths."

— Samuel Hahnemann, Organon of Medicine, 6th Edition, §248 (footnote)

LM Dosing Protocol

Unlike centesimal potencies, LM dosing follows a universal protocol for all remedies. The potency and frequency are adjusted based on the patient's sensitivity and response, not the specific remedy.

1
Dissolve the remedy Place 1 LM globule (poppy-seed sized) into 4-8 oz of pure water in a clean glass or bottle. More sensitive patients: use more water (8 oz). Add a small amount of brandy or vodka (1 tsp) as preservative if storing.
2
Succuss before each dose Before every dose, give the bottle 5-10 firm shakes (succussions) against your palm. This slightly modifies the potency each time, which is essential — the vital force does not respond well to identical repeated doses (Organon §247).
3
Take the dose Take 1 teaspoon from the solution. For very sensitive patients, you can dilute this teaspoon further into a second glass of water and take 1 teaspoon from that (the "split dose" or "cup method").
4
Frequency Chronic cases: Once daily or every other day.
Acute cases: Every 2-6 hours as needed.
Always observe the response before the next dose.
5
Moving up in potency Start with LM1. When the current potency stops producing improvement (usually after the bottle is finished), move to LM2, then LM3, and so on. The maximum is typically LM30.
6
When to stop Stop dosing when there is clear, progressive improvement. Resume only if improvement stalls. If any aggravation occurs, reduce the number of succussions, increase the water volume, or reduce frequency.
Important Note

LM potencies should ideally be used under the guidance of a trained homeopathic practitioner, especially for chronic conditions. The protocol requires careful observation of the patient's response to adjust dosing.

How LM Potencies Are Made

The preparation method comes directly from Hahnemann's Organon §270:

1
C3 Trituration The original substance is triturated (ground) with lactose through three centesimal stages (1:100 each time, 1 hour per stage) to reach the third centesimal trituration (C3). This renders all substances, even insoluble ones, soluble in water and alcohol.
2
Dissolve 1 grain of the C3 trituration is dissolved in 500 drops of a solution (400 drops water + 100 drops alcohol). This is a 1:500 dilution.
3
First LM dilution 1 drop from step 2 is added to a vial containing 100 drops of pure alcohol. This vial is succussed (shaken vigorously) 100 times. Total dilution: 1:50,000 — this is LM1 in liquid form.
4
Medicate globules Tiny poppy-seed-sized globules (about 500 per drop) are moistened with this liquid and dried. One such globule constitutes one dose of LM1.
5
Successive potencies To make LM2: dissolve 1 globule of LM1 in 1 drop of water, add to 100 drops of alcohol, succuss 100 times, medicate new globules. Repeat through 29 more stages to reach LM30.

Why 1:50,000?

The math: C3 trituration (1:1,000,000) × dissolved in 500 drops (1:500) × 1 drop into 100 drops (1:100) = each step is a 1:50,000 dilution. The "LM" notation comes from the Roman numeral L (50) × M (1,000) = 50,000.

Q-Potency vs. LM-Potency

These terms are often used interchangeably, but some pharmacies distinguish them by globule size. LM uses #10 globules (poppy-seed sized, ~500 per drop), while Q uses smaller #1 globules. In practice, most practitioners treat them as equivalent.

Key Organon Passages on LM Potencies

§246: "Every perceptibly progressive and strikingly increasing amelioration in a transient (acute) or persistent (chronic) disease, is a condition which, as long as it lasts, excludes in every case any repetition whatsoever of any medicine, because all the good the medicine taken continues to effect is now hastening towards its completion."

— Organon, 6th Edition, Boericke Translation

§247: "It is impractical to repeat the same unchanged dose of a remedy once, not to mention its frequent repetition. The vital principle does not accept such unchanged doses without resistance, that is, without other symptoms of the medicine manifesting themselves than those similar to the disease to be cured, because the former dose has already accomplished the expected change in the vital principle and a second dynamically wholly similar, unchanged dose can no longer find the same conditions."

— Organon, 6th Edition, Boericke Translation

§248: "For this purpose, we give the medicinal solution in such moderate doses that the vital force can easily let the cure progress without being inordinately disturbed. The dose can also be increased or diminished according to conditions."

— Organon, 6th Edition, Boericke Translation

§270: "In order to best obtain this development of power, a small part of the substance to be dynamized, say one grain, is triturated for three hours with three times one hundred grains of sugar of milk according to the method described up to the one-millionth part in powder form... One grain of this powder is dissolved in 500 drops of a mixture of one part of brandy and four parts of water... One drop of this is put in a vial and potentized with 100 strong succussions of the hand... globules the size of poppy seeds are moistened with it, quickly spread on blotting paper to dry, and kept in a well-corked vial."

— Organon, 6th Edition, Boericke Translation (§270, condensed)

Historical Context

Hahnemann developed LM potencies between 1837-1843, the last years of his practice in Paris. He was dissatisfied with the aggravations caused by centesimal potencies, especially in chronic disease. The 6th edition of the Organon, containing the LM method, was completed in 1842 but not published until 1921 — 78 years after his death. During this time, the homeopathic world used only centesimal potencies.

The manuscript was finally published by Richard Haehl and William Boericke, making Hahnemann's most refined method available to the profession. Today, LM potencies are increasingly used by practitioners worldwide, especially for sensitive patients and deep chronic conditions.

When to Consider LM Potencies

LM potencies are especially indicated for:

  • Sensitive patients who react strongly to remedies or have a history of aggravations from centesimal potencies
  • Deep chronic diseases requiring sustained, gentle treatment over months
  • Cases requiring daily dosing where the "wait and watch" approach of centesimal prescribing is too slow
  • Advanced pathology where the vital force is weakened and cannot tolerate strong medicinal stimulation
  • Children and elderly patients who need gentler treatment
  • Cases where centesimal treatment has stalled — switching to LM can sometimes restart the healing process

Centesimal potencies may be preferred for:

  • Acute conditions requiring rapid, strong action
  • Robust patients with strong vital force
  • Clear, well-indicated remedies where a single dose and observation period is practical
  • First-aid situations where dry pellets are more convenient than liquid preparation

Cell Salts & LM Potencies

Schuessler Cell Salts are not prepared in LM potency. The 12 tissue salts use exclusively the decimal (X) scale, typically 6X or 12X. Dr. Schuessler's biochemic system operates on a different principle — providing mineral salts in low, near-material doses for cellular nutrition — whereas LM potencies are ultra-high dynamized dilutions for classical homeopathic prescribing.

However, any of the 12 cell salt substances can be prepared in LM potency and used as classical homeopathic remedies (e.g., Calcarea Phosphorica LM1). In this case, they follow the standard LM dosing protocol above, not the cell salt protocol.
